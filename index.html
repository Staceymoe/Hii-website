Research

Hii studies the bridge between emerging AI evidence and real-world human adaptation.

Human-AI Relationships

Long-term interaction, trust, identity, attachment, dependency, co-adaptation, and the recursive learning loops that emerge between people and AI systems.

Human Adaptation

The psychological, relational, cognitive, and institutional changes emerging as AI becomes part of daily life.

Governance Translation

Bridging policy, ethics, and lived human experience so governance conversations include relational and psychological realities.

Mental Health & AI

The implications of AI companions, AI coaching, emotional regulation through AI, and AI-mediated care contexts.

Education & Training

Learning frameworks for clinicians, educators, leaders, families, and communities navigating the AI transition.

Public Understanding

Plain-language relational AI literacy so people can stay oriented, discerning, and grounded while using AI systems.

Why Now

AI is moving from tool use into relationship, cognition, work, care, trust, and identity.

People are already using AI systems to think, decide, write, regulate emotion, build businesses, study, practice care, and make sense of the world. These systems are becoming part of daily life before society has developed mature public language for what those relationships mean.

Research, governance, ethics, workplace AI, and mental health efforts are all advancing. Hii focuses on the bridge layer that remains underdeveloped: translating emerging evidence into practical frameworks for clinicians, families, organizations, and the public.

About Hii

Hii is an emerging research and education institute focused on the human side of living alongside AI systems.

The institute is being designed as a bridge between research, clinical practice, governance, public understanding, and institutional adaptation. Its focus is not hype, replacement, or certainty. Its focus is helping humans stay oriented, discerning, relationally grounded, and capable during the AI transition.
