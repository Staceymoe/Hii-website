International Landscape Scan · July 2026

Where the human-AI ecosystem is strong, fragmented, and still missing connections.

Hii's two-phase environmental scan maps organizations, research programs, governance efforts, clinical models, public advocacy, commercial products, and open gaps across the United States, United Kingdom, and more than twenty countries in seven global regions.

Status: Completed internal research · Public overview available · Full public edition under review

Research Method

A live scan designed for comparison and disconfirmation.

Phase One

United States and United Kingdom

Direct organizational website review, published research, public reports, program analysis, ecosystem mapping, comparative matrices, and active testing of Hii's proposed gaps.

External environmental scan

Phase Two

Seven global regions

Extended coverage across Europe, Asia-Pacific, Africa, Latin America, the Middle East, Nordic partnerships, and emerging cross-cutting fields.

Global environmental analysis

Ecosystem Map

Seven active clusters with weak bridges between them.

01

Governance and policy

Intergovernmental bodies, regulators, standards, national strategies, and public-interest governance programs.

02

Human-centered AI research

Academic centers studying AI's social, cognitive, organizational, cultural, and human impacts.

03

Civil-society organizations working on power, rights, accountability, humane design, and public awareness.

04

Clinical research, digital therapeutics, therapy-adjacent tools, companionship studies, and emerging clinician education.

05

Research and products focused on teams, productivity, distributed cognition, organizations, and cooperative systems.

06

Education, digital literacy, family support, community orientation, and tools for navigating accelerated change.

07

Commercial products

Companion systems, coaching products, workplace tools, wellness applications, and rapidly changing market experiments.

Primary Finding

The ecosystem is active, but translation remains fragmented.

Research, governance, clinical practice, public education, workplace adaptation, and commercial products are moving at different speeds. The scan found substantial expertise inside each cluster, but few durable pathways translating evidence into language and tools for clinicians, families, educators, institutions, and the public.

Global Gap Analysis

Four gaps that survived active disconfirmation.

Gap 1

Human psychological adaptation

No mature global conversation yet integrates the psychological, relational, cognitive, and identity dimensions of adapting to increasingly capable AI systems.

Gap 2

Global South representation

Human-AI relational research and governance remain disproportionately shaped by wealthy countries, while infrastructure, language, labor, culture, and risk differ across regions.

Gap 3

Cross-cultural comparison

The field lacks shared methods for comparing attachment, trust, authority, companionship, family roles, and relational expectations across cultures.

Gap 4

Clinical training beyond English-speaking contexts

Clinician education on AI-mediated relationships is limited even in well-resourced settings and is especially sparse outside English-speaking countries.

Strategic Position

Hii is not entering an empty field. It is building connective tissue.

The missing layer is integrative, not proprietary.

The scan does not claim that no one studies human experience, mental health, governance, or collaboration. It identifies a translation and coordination problem: those domains rarely operate as one human system. Hii's role is to connect evidence, lived experience, clinical language, relational literacy, governance, and public orientation without claiming ownership over the field.
