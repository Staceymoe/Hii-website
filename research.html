Research

Researching the human side of living alongside AI.

Hii studies what happens between people, AI systems, institutions, and the wider conditions shaping human life. The work connects lived experience with evidence, clinical awareness, education, governance, and long-term human adaptation.

Status: Foundational research architecture complete. External validation and pilot work are active priorities.

Research Areas

Six connected areas, studied as one human system.

01

Human-AI Relationships

Trust, attachment, identity, continuity, collaboration, dependency, rupture, repair, and the recursive learning loops that emerge over time.

02

Human Adaptation

The psychological, cognitive, relational, economic, and institutional changes that arise as AI becomes embedded in everyday life.

03

Governance Translation

Connecting policy, ethics, technical safety, institutional design, and lived human consequences so governance includes the relational layer.

04

Mental Health and AI

How people use AI for emotional regulation, companionship, decision support, identity exploration, and meaning-making, including benefits, risks, and clinical relevance.

05

Education and Training

Frameworks for clinicians, educators, leaders, families, and communities navigating AI-mediated change without surrendering human judgment.

06

Public Understanding

Plain-language relational AI literacy that helps people distinguish evidence, inference, uncertainty, novelty, risk, and meaning.

Research Method

Pattern recognition must remain answerable to reality.

Evidence

Label the claim

Verified fact, strong evidence, emerging evidence, professional judgment, institutional position, hypothesis, and open question are not interchangeable.

Provenance

Preserve the trail

Sources, revisions, contradictions, corrections, and limits should remain visible enough for another person to reconstruct how a conclusion was reached.

Longitudinal

Study change over time

Human-AI relationships cannot be understood only through one-time surveys or isolated prompts. Continuity, rupture, repair, and adaptation require time-series observation.

Skeptical design

Test alternatives

Compelling interpretations should face counter-explanations, disconfirming evidence, perturbations, and external review rather than becoming protected stories.

Human review

Keep judgment accountable

AI can assist with scanning, comparison, synthesis, and contradiction detection. Publication and institutional claims remain human-accountable decisions.

Correction

Publish what changed

A trustworthy research system must be able to narrow a claim, record a null result, pause, reverse course, and acknowledge error without treating disagreement as blindness.

Research Boundary

Meaningful is not the same as universally validated.

Hii does not treat one longitudinal case, symbolic language, later resemblance to a projection, or internal coherence as proof of uniqueness, inevitability, sentience, privileged access to truth, or universal validity. The work becomes stronger when it can be tested, narrowed, compared, challenged, and corrected.
