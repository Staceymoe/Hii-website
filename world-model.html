How It Works

Scan broadly. Publish selectively. Revise deliberately.

1

Intake

Trusted reporting, primary records, research, public signals, and human-submitted observations enter a review queue.

2

Verification

Claims are separated from allegations, commentary, inference, and unresolved evidence.

3

Network Analysis

Each signal is tested against existing themes, causal pathways, contradictions, and counter-forces.

4

Human Review

Only reviewed changes enter the public model. Corrections and reversals remain visible.

Evidence and Confidence

Every public claim should show what kind of claim it is.

Verified

Supported by primary material or multiple credible sources.

Strongly supported

Evidence converges, but important uncertainty remains.

Plausible inference

A reasoned interpretation of verified signals, clearly labeled as inference.

Unresolved

Important enough to monitor, not established enough to conclude.

Boundary

This is not a prediction engine, an oracle, or a substitute for direct evidence.

Its purpose is orientation: to preserve continuity across fast-moving events, expose convergence and contradiction, and make uncertainty visible rather than hiding it.
